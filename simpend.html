  • A simple pendulum consists of a (point) mass attached to the end of a string, the other end of which is fixed to a stationary object.  In its equilibrium position the string hangs vertically with tension equal to mg.  If the mass is displaced, such that the string remains taut and the angle between the string and the vertical (theta) is small, then released the pendulum will move in SHM.

  • So long as the angular displacement is small (which means the motion is SHM) the period is independent of the displacement.  This means that the simple pendulum can be used as a time keeping device , as in for example, the grandfather clock .

    •  
  • "Small" is of course a relative term when applied to displacment.  As an example if the angle theta is less than about 20o the SHM prediction of the period is good to about 2%.
