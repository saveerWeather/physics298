            WORK      

 

  • Qualitative:  Work is a measure of the "change" a force gives rise to when it acts on an object.

 

  • CONSTANT FORCE:  The work done by a constant force acting on an object is equal to the scalar product of the force and the displacement.





      This can be thought of as either,

·        Magnitude of the force times the component of displacement along F or,

·        Magnitude of the displacement times the component of the force along r.

Work is a scalar quantity.

  •    If an applied force causes no displacement, then no work is done.
    This is in contrast to your everyday understanding of work.  If you spend all day pushing on a wall, you will feel as if you have done a lot of work - in fact no work will have been done, since the wall did not move.

 

  •    If an applied force and the displacement are perpendicular, then no work is done.
    This is the situation in uniform circular motion.  The displacement is tangential, the centripetal force is radial.  Therefore, the centripetal force does no work.

  •    A force can do a negative amount of work.

If the angle between the force and the displacement is greater than 900 and less than 1800 then W < 0. For example the work done by the gravitational force when an object is raised through a height, h. The work done by the gravitational force is –mgh. Work done against gravity is dependent only on height and weight. Same amount of work is done against gravity when climbing one floor via stairs and climbing one floor via elevator. Unfortunately you do the work when you use the stairs, whereas the elevator motor does the work when you use the elevator !

  • Units of Power
    • SI            -    1 Watt = 1 Joule / second  (J/s)
    • British      -    1 ft.pound = 1 foot. pound/second  (ft.lb/s)
    • Conversions
      1 kilowatt (kW) = 1000  Watts
      1 horse power (hp) = 550 ft.lb/s = 746 Watts
    •  Since Work = Power x time we can use the unit kilowatt.hours as a unit of work.  This is the unit which electric companies in the USA use to charge for electricity.
