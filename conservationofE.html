  • Conservation of Energy 


One of the most important basic principles of Science, which is obeyed with no exceptions.
 

"The total energy in an isolated system is constant"

However, "Energy may be converted from one form to another within that system"

For example, when falling to earth, an object converts gravitational potential energy to kinetic energy.  Just before it hits the earth, all available potential energy has been converted to kinetic energy.  The kinetic energy is then converted to heat, sound and possibly other forms of potential energy as the object rapidly comes to rest.  In all cases, since energy is conserved, we may state,

"Initial energy = Final energy"
