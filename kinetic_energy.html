  • When a constant force is applied to an object initially at rest it can be shown that the work done by the force in accelerating the object to velocity v is given by the change on the kinetic energy.

This is known as the Work-Energy theorem.

Note that the work done can be negative, this indicates that the force doing work opposes the motion of the particle.

  • Kinetic energy has magnitude only, it is a scalar quantity.
  • Since the velocity of an object is measured "relative" to some other "fixed" object, the magnitude of the kinetic energy of an object is not constant, but depends on the chosen reference frame.  Usually we choose the reference frame in which the earth is at rest. 
  • Heat energy may be considered as the kinetic energy of the atoms or molecules making up the object.
